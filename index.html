Apo Reef
www.apo-reef.com
Apo Reef is the largest coral reef in the Philippines! It spreads over an area of 34 square kilometres, making it the second largest connecting coral reef in the world (the first place being occupied by the Great Barrier Reef of Australia). The Apo Reef, also considered as the second largest atoll-like reef in the Philippines comprises of two isolated coral reefs that are disconnected by a 30-meter deep channel. The clear blue waters of the channel are teeming with 285 species of colourful marine life, including tropical aquarium fish, snappers and the crevice-dwelling moray.
The health of your fish depends upon having water in the tank that is healthy for them to live in. Monitor the water quality, including pH, ammonia and nitrite levels by using an at- home test kit, or submit a water sample for testing at your local fish or exotic pet shop.
